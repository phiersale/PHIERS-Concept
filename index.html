PHIERSale
Cooperative marketplace using collective bargaining on medications — generating $50+/month in savings per member. That $50 sponsors a Medicaid beneficiary into telehealth, saving CMS $9,400/year. Those savings sponsor more people. The cycle builds until coverage reaches everyone.

Job training and placement that strengthens the safety net while providing all workers with real benefits — creating good jobs in rural areas, urban centers, and everywhere in between.

Resource matching and referral connecting people to the care, services, and support they need — wherever they are.
Together these three components create a safety net for the safety net — universal coverage, paid for entirely by the savings the system itself generates.

What Is the Survey?

The PHIERS.org survey lets constituents communicate their priorities directly to elected officials.

When 1,500 people in a district complete the survey, their representative is summoned to a town hall where they must publicly address those demands and commit to acting on them.

Candidates seeking office must commit to those demands before November 2026 — publicly, on the record.

This is direct democracy in action.

How Does Replacement Work?

When an elected official refuses to respond to constituent demands, PHIERS.org organizes the district to pursue every available constitutional tool — primary challenges, special elections, and organized constituent campaigns — to replace them with someone who will do the job.

We do not wait for the next scheduled election to apply pressure.

Organized constituents with documented demands are the most powerful force in any district. That power begins the moment you sign.
