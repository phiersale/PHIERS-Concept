Quick 10‑second explainer

PHIERS converts telehealth savings into universal coverage. Telehealth ≈ $600 vs traditional insurance ≈ $8,000. The freed savings fund the next wave — an exponential cascade to coverage.

Why this matters

  • Lower costs for routine care
  • Coverage for millions without new taxes
  • Jobs and local investment

The Crisis

Congress let subsidies expire — 22 million lost coverage
Prices are spiking and coverage is dropping. We have a narrow window to force action before the system destabilizes. Organized constituent pressure works — 3.5% participation triggers town halls and policy change.

What this means for people

  • Higher premiums and fewer options
  • More uncompensated ER care and taxpayer burden
  • Millions at risk of losing access to routine care

How It Works — The Exponential Mechanism

Core math

Traditional insurance: $8,000+ / year. Telehealth alternative: $600 / year. That frees roughly $7,400 per person — savings that fund additional conversions and create an exponential cascade.

1 → 12 → 148 → 1,825 → 22,508 → 302K → 3.7M → 46M → 120M

5D Solutions — One solution, five outcomes

PHIERS addresses healthcare access, economic security, dignified work, protection from extraction, and sustainable communities — together these create a resilient system.

  • Healthcare: Universal coverage at point of service
  • Economy: Millions of AI-resistant jobs
  • Community: Local investment and case management
  • Democracy: Constituent power forcing policy change
  • Justice: No new taxes; funded from savings
