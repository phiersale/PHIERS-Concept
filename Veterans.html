The Betrayal They're Hiding

While You Were Serving,
They Were Planning This.

The same people beating the drums of war are quietly dismantling the only system meant to care for you when you come home.

The VA just tried to slip a rule into the Federal Register — effective immediately, no public notice — that would have cut disability benefits for veterans who take medication. A veteran with 100% PTSD rating could have dropped to 30% simply because their medication was working.

They backed off after 10,000+ public comments in 60 hours. But the rule is still on the books. They can reinstate it any time.

30,000 VA staff are being cut — the people who process your claims, schedule your appointments, answer your calls. Front-line workers. Gone.

Nursing homes closing. Mental health positions eliminated. Rural clinics shutting down. Urban clinics so overwhelmed they're turning away non-emergency care.

And while they gut the VA, they're building up for the largest military deployment since Iraq. Two carrier strike groups. Hundreds of fighter jets. A new generation of Americans about to be sent into combat. They'll come home to a VA that no longer exists.

Why the Founder of PHIERS Left the VA

Will Price — Chief Solutions Architect

Twenty years inside the VA system. Senior Analyst on the Electronic Health Record Modernization program. Registration, eligibility, and enrollment systems. VistA. The actual machinery that determines whether veterans receive care — or fall through the cracks.

One of five civilians selected for the VA's first enterprise-wide reengineering initiative. Worked directly with the Office of the CIO. Saw how the system actually worked — and who it actually served.

He watched the same problems repeat for two decades. The same budget fights. The same staffing shortages. The same veterans falling through the same cracks — because the architecture was never designed to catch everyone.

He quit to build the alternative.

The Math That Changes Everything

$600 vs $10,000+

$600 per year covers roughly 80% of what veterans actually need — primary care, mental health, prescriptions, chronic condition management. $10,000+ per year is what traditional insurance costs for the same care.

That difference creates a cascade: one veteran switching from insurance to PHIERS saves enough to cover 12 more. Those 12 save enough to cover 148. The cycle compounds.

$2.73 trillion in annual savings. Universal coverage for every American — including every veteran — in 8–13 months.
