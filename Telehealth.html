The Simple Truth

About 80% of what people need is basic, everyday care.

Yet everyone pays as if they're going to the hospital every year.

Here's the real breakdown:

  • Insurance charges about $10,000 a year to run routine care through a maze of paperwork, pre-approvals, and claim denials.
  • Telehealth covers the same everyday care for about $600 — same doctors, same care, just without the middlemen taking a cut.

That's not fixing the broken system. That's a completely different system.

What This Means for Your Family

Imagine calling a doctor without worrying about the bill.

Imagine not losing half your raise to premiums.

Imagine switching jobs without risking your family's coverage.

Imagine getting care the same day instead of waiting three weeks.

That's what $600 makes possible.

Why Insurance Costs Exploded

Insurance was built for one thing: the rare, expensive disaster. A surgery. A hospitalization. Events that happen once or twice in a lifetime.

But somewhere along the way it became the only door to all healthcare. Doctor visits. Prescription refills. Therapy. A checkup.

Things that have nothing to do with disaster are now routed through a system designed for disaster.

That's why a basic doctor visit can cost hundreds. That's why premiums keep climbing.

When you price routine care like it's open-heart surgery, it eventually costs like open-heart surgery.

You've been paying $10,000 for a door you barely use. Most people only ever needed the $600 door.

What Telehealth Actually Gives You

Telehealth is direct care. No middleman. No pre-approval. No "out of network." No denials. Just a doctor — 24/7, whenever you need one.

It covers the things people actually use:

  • regular doctor visits and urgent care
  • mental health and counseling
  • managing conditions like diabetes or high blood pressure
  • prescription refills
  • preventive care and basic labs
  • guidance to the right specialist when needed

No waiting rooms. No taking half a day off work. No surprise bills.

Telehealth covers about 80% of what Americans need — at roughly one-fourteenth the cost of insurance.

Keep Insurance for the Big Stuff

Insurance still matters — for the 20%. Hospitalizations. Major surgery. Serious emergencies.

Those are rare, but when they happen they're financially devastating. Insurance is the right tool for that — and only that.

PHIERS doesn't eliminate insurance. It puts insurance back where it belongs.

📱 Telehealth — The 80%

Everything you use day to day. About $600 a year. Direct access. No gatekeepers.

🏥 Insurance — The 20%

The catastrophic backstop. Lower cost because it's no longer covering your annual checkup.

When you stop using a sledgehammer for everything, a lot of things get cheaper.

When healthcare drops from $10,000 to $600, families finally get breathing room.

Bills get lighter. Paychecks go further. Life stops feeling like a constant emergency.

That's what stabilization looks like.

What Happens When This Changes

When telehealth handles everyday care, the whole system gets cheaper. Automatically. At every level.

  • Fewer ER visits — people stop using emergency rooms for things a telehealth doctor can handle in 10 minutes
  • Fewer insurance claims — fewer crises mean fewer catastrophic payouts
  • Lower premiums, Medicare, and Medicaid costs — public programs stabilize
  • Lower taxpayer subsidies — we spend less to cover the same people

At national scale, that frees up about $2.7 trillion a year that's currently disappearing into insurance bureaucracy.

That money doesn't vanish. It flows back into the real economy: wages, local businesses, housing, public investment, community stability.

Congress Knows This Works

This isn't a secret. Insurance companies and drug companies spend hundreds of millions making sure nothing changes. A $600 telehealth option threatens their profits directly.

Congress already has the authority under the ACA to make this happen. No new law. No new agency. One vote.

The problem isn't the idea — it's the politics. They haven't done it because nobody has organized enough people to force the issue.

That's what PHIERS changes.

Healthcare is where we prove the power works. Everything else is what we do with the proof.
