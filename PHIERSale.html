16 Years of Proof

This Isn't Theory.
It Already Works.

We've already done the hardest part. Now it scales.

In 2009, Will Price and Al Wilson began designing a cooperative purchasing system for medications — buying directly from manufacturers, eliminating every layer of markup between the factory and the patient.

No law was required. No congressional approval. No insurance company's permission. Just organized buyers with collective leverage — and suppliers willing to deal direct.

The result: drug costs cut by 80% or more for participating members.

The same principle that works for medications works for groceries, household goods, and everything else you buy. The suppliers are the same. The markups are optional. The profit motive is the only thing we remove.

How the Cooperative Works

One Membership.
Wholesale Prices.

PHIERSale works because organized buyers have leverage that individual consumers never will. Here's the sequence:

1

Members pool their purchasing power

When thousands of people buy the same medications, groceries, and essentials through one system, suppliers compete for that volume — not the other way around.

2

We buy direct from manufacturers and suppliers

The same factories and distributors that supply Target, Walmart, and CVS supply us — minus every layer of markup, broker fees, and shareholder profit that inflates your bill.

3

Every penny of savings returns to members

No profit motive. No investors to pay. No executive bonuses. The savings that would have gone to a corporation go back into your pocket — or fund the next member's coverage.

4

Each member sponsors more coverage

$50/month savings per medication member sponsors one Medicaid beneficiary into telehealth — saving CMS $9,400/year. That cycle builds until universal coverage pays for itself.

→
One member saves $50/month on medications
→
That $50 sponsors a Medicaid beneficiary into telehealth
→
CMS saves $9,400/year per sponsored member
→
Savings compound. Coverage expands. It snowballs fast.

The Bigger Picture

This Is What
Economic Power
Actually Looks Like.

Protests signal displeasure. Boycotts create temporary disruption. PHIERSale creates a permanent alternative that undercuts corporations at the source — not by asking them to change, but by making them irrelevant.

The strategic logic:

When enough members buy medications through PHIERSale instead of CVS or Walgreens, those chains lose revenue. When enough members buy groceries through PHIERSale instead of Kroger or Walmart, those chains face real competition for the first time. We don't lobby for lower prices. We create them.

This is also how PHIERSale funds everything else. Those savings don't just lower costs — they create the surplus that can fund universal healthcare, jobs, and long-term economic stability for every American.

Universal coverage paid for by savings already being wasted. No new taxes. No new costs. Just money that was going to corporate profit — redirected to people.
