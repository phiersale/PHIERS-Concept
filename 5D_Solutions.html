The 5D Plan

Five things that work better together — because your life works that way too.

Think of it like your household budget. When the rent goes up, something else gets cut. When healthcare eats your paycheck, the car repair waits. When the car doesn't get fixed, you lose the job. It's all connected — and you can't fix one thing without the others moving too.

They break together — they have to be fixed together.

5D at a Glance

🏥 Healthcare

Cut your bill from $10,000 to $600. Real doctors 24/7. No waiting rooms. No gatekeepers. No surprise bills.

💼 Jobs

Millions of jobs AI can't take — home health aides, community health workers, elder care. Real work. Real wages.

💰 Economy

$2.73 trillion a year freed up — not from new taxes, but from stopping the waste. That money funds affordable housing, higher wages, and a basic income floor so nobody hits the ground with nothing.

⚡ Political Power

When workers don't need their employer for healthcare, unions negotiate wages. When voters are organized, Congress listens.

🕊️ Peace

Rules that actually protect regular people — not the corporations running the system. Guardrails that keep the powerful accountable.

1.

This Is the Only Way It Works

The crisis is structural.

The response has to be too.

Fixing one thing at a time while everything else is on fire doesn't work. We fix them together — because that's the only way any of it actually sticks.

Not because it's ambitious. Because it's necessary.

2.

Why You Can't Fix One Thing Without the Others

Fix healthcare and workers stop being trapped in jobs they hate just to keep their insurance. Free people from that trap and wages go up. Wages go up and families can afford rent. Families afford rent and communities get stronger.

It all moves together — or it all falls together.

3.

We Fix Them Together — Not One at a Time

The old way: Pass one bill. Debate the next. Fund one program. Delay the next.

But the problems don't wait for each other — and neither can the solutions. We do them together:

Fix healthcare. Create real jobs. Put the savings back in people's pockets. Give workers more power. Make the rules actually protect regular people.

Each piece strengthens the others.

This is how you actually fix things.

4.

Where We Start

Healthcare is where we begin — because it's the biggest drain on working people's budgets and the one Congress has the clearest authority to fix. Win that and everything loosens up. Workers can move jobs. Wages can go up. Families can afford rent. Communities can rebuild.

Each win makes the next one easier.

5.

Why This Is Achievable

This doesn't require new taxes. It doesn't require permission. It requires taking back money that's already yours.

Telehealth reduces annual healthcare costs from $10,000 to $600. That's not a theory — that's the price difference. The savings fund everything else.

The math works. The savings are real. This is already paid for — we just have to take it back.

What This Actually Pays For

We're talking about real things. Your rent. Your grocery bill. Your kid's student loans. Childcare that doesn't eat your whole paycheck. A retirement that doesn't require working until you drop.

These aren't abstract policy goals — they're what $2.73 trillion in freed-up money can actually pay for. It's already being collected. It's just going to the wrong place.

6.

The Five Dimensions

Each one is practical. Each one reinforces the others. Each one is achievable. None works alone.

1. Healthcare

Cut your bill from $10,000 to $600. Get a real doctor 24/7. No waiting rooms. No gatekeepers. No surprise bills. This is the door — everything else walks through it.

2. Jobs

Create millions of jobs that AI can't take: home health aides, community health workers, elder care, mental health support. Real work. Real wages. Can't be outsourced. Can't be automated.

3. Economy

$2.73 trillion a year freed up — not from new taxes, but from stopping the waste. That funds affordable housing, higher wages, and a basic income floor so nobody hits the ground with nothing. Your money, back where it belongs.

4. Political Power

When workers don't need their employer for healthcare, unions negotiate from strength. When voters are organized, Congress answers to us. Power shifts back to people.

5. Peace

Rules that actually protect regular people — not the corporations running the system. Guardrails that keep the powerful accountable to everyone else. A country that works for us.

Together, they work. Separately, none of them stick.

7.

What This Changes

Here's what the chain actually looks like in real life:

Healthcare gets affordable → you're not chained to a job you hate just for insurance → your union negotiates wages instead of defending benefits → your paycheck goes up → you can actually afford rent → your kids go to a fully funded school → your neighborhood gets better → your community gets stronger.

Every link in that chain is real. Every one of them is achievable. And every one of them starts with fixing healthcare first.

The money is already there. We just have to take it back.
