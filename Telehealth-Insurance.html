Telehealth vs Insurance

The System Rigged. The Solution Simple.

Telehealth makes care cheaper and easier. Sadly, insurance rules often block savings and care from reaching people it should protect.

Making matters worse, most Americans think "healthcare = insurance."

They're not entirely wrong. They're not entirely right either.

The Problem We're Solving

Most Americans think "healthcare = insurance" — but insurance is only designed for catastrophic events (hospitalization, specialty care, etc.).

Meanwhile, 80% of what people actually need is basic, everyday care. Insurance plans ofte block access to the care and refuse to pay thier part. We can do better.

Roftunately, 80% of the insured never use hospitalization or specialty care. These healthcare consumers are the perfect candidate for telehealht.

There's only one solution: These two should be completely different.

PHIERS fixes this by separating care from catastrophe.

The 80/20 Healthcare Model: Separate Care from Catastrophe Protection

What Telehealth Actually Does

Telehealth isn't insurance — it's direct care.

It covers the things people use constantly:

  • Primary care
  • Urgent care
  • Mental health
  • Chronic disease management
  • Prescription refills
  • Preventive care
  • Basic labs
  • Care navigation
  • Remote counseling

Telehealth handles 80% of healthcare needs at 1/14th the cost.

No claims. No networks. No middlemen. No billing codes. No prior authorization.

Just care.

What Insurance Is Actually For

Insurance is a financial product, not a care system.

It exists to protect people from:

  • Hospitalizations
  • Surgeries
  • Emergency trauma
  • Long-term rehab
  • Expensive imaging
  • Catastrophic events

These events are rare — but extremely costly.

Insurance is the backstop, not the front door.

Why America Needs Both

When telehealth handles the 80%:

  • ✓ ER visits drop
  • ✓ Hospital readmits drop
  • ✓ Claims drop
  • ✓ Premiums drop
  • ✓ Medicaid costs drop
  • ✓ Medicare costs drop
  • ✓ ACA subsidies drop
  • ✓ Private insurance stabilizes

This is a positive savings cascade and its what PHIERS is here to provide.

Telehealth climbs the cost of the system, and Insurance protects against the worst events.

Together, they create a system that is:

  • Affordable — 80% cost reduction + cascade savings
  • Scalable — Works for 234M Americans
  • Defensible — Market-based, not government mandated
  • Universal — Accessible to everyone

The Key Insight

Telehealth is care. Insurance is protection. PHIERS is the Co-op that finally combines them.

This is the foundation of everything PHIERS builds. Once you separate care from catastrophe, the math becomes simple. Universal coverage becomes possible.
